VERA Creed
===========

**Accessibility** and **Integrity** are guiding principles that shape both the VERA platform and the research community it supports.

**Accessibility**:

  I will practice transparency and collaboration in my research relationships and practices, recognizing VERA’s focus on community.

**Integrity**:

  I will strive to act mindfully and ethically, actively cultivating VERA values (identified in the Code of Conduct) and the structures (social and technological) that empower my consistent alignment with those values.

VERA Code of Conduct
---------------------

The values shaping our Code of Conduct reinforce commitment to VERA’s guiding principles by the community of researchers and research participants. As a researcher in the VERA community, I will:

**Be Transparent**  

  ...in my methods and experiments.  

  ...in my approach to data privacy and the mitigation of risks.  

  ...about my purpose and perspective in doing XR research or using XR technologies in my research.  

  ...in my relationships with research colleagues and participants.  

  ...about actual and perceived conflicts of interest and conflicts of commitment to corporate, institutional, financial, or governmental entities, and to specific products or services.

**Be Collaborative**  

  ...by supporting the personal and professional growth of marginalized, underprivileged, and emerging researchers.  

  ...by thoughtfully embargoing research while keeping in mind the goal of sharing data and results with the VERA community.  

  ...by prioritizing collaboration over competition.  

  ...by contributing to a trusting environment that avoids poaching unpublished ideas, data, and conclusions from others.  

  ...by supporting and protecting spaces that encourage the development and sharing of new ideas.  

  ...by working to document and remember the genealogy of ideas and contributors along the way, and ensuring to cite studies and research influences.  

  ...by accepting and giving constructive criticism and engagement with ideas.  

  ...by ensuring that each research participant is sufficiently informed such that they can fully consent to research projects.

**Be Mindful**  

  ...about external stressors that can affect research integrity, including money, time, and professional prominence.  

  ...in protecting participants, researchers, and VERA community members by respecting their choices, data, and contributions.  

  ...of individual responsibility and accountability for yourself and others in the VERA community.  

  ...by striving to respect the diversity of persons and ideas.  

  ...in recognizing that you are a steward of participant data that requires protection and care even after your study has concluded.

**Be Ethical**  

  ...by seeking to not only avoid harms but actively pursue good ends for other researchers, participants, and the broader community. 

  ...by designing and conducting robust studies that strive to answer meaningful questions, mitigate risks, and produce knowledge for the XR community’s benefit.  

  ...by operating with the premise that legal standards and institutional regulations are baselines for XR best practices, and must sometimes be surpassed to ensure ethical operations.  
  
  ...by seeking positive outcomes for technological accessibility, future research, and the goals of academic and social communities through research.
